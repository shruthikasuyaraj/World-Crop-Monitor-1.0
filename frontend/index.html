Methodology

Our Crop Stress Index combines multiple indicators into a composite score (0-100):

  • Drought Index (40%): Based on rainfall deficit and soil moisture indicators
  • Heat Index (30%): Temperature anomalies above optimal crop growth thresholds
  • Vegetation Stress (30%): NDVI anomalies and vegetation health indicators

About World Crop Monitor

Our mission to prevent food crises through early intervention

Our Mission

World Crop Monitor addresses the global food security crisis by providing early visibility into crop stress conditions before food shortages escalate. We consolidate fragmented climate and satellite data into actionable early warnings for farmers, NGOs, and early-response organizations.

Why Early Warning Matters

Food insecurity does not occur suddenly. Crop stress due to drought, heat, and abnormal weather patterns appears weeks in advance. By detecting these early indicators, we can enable proactive intervention rather than reactive emergency response.

Target Users

  • Smallholder farmers seeking climate-adaptive guidance
  • NGOs planning humanitarian response and food aid distribution
  • Governments and policymakers managing food security strategies
  • Research organizations studying climate-agriculture interactions

Expected Impact

Early Warning Detection weeks before crisis escalation
